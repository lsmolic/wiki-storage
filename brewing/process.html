Rice Growing

Even before a single kura-bito puts their hands on a single grain of rice, there are many farmers, companies, organizations, and even governments involved in producing rice that will eventually end up in a sake brewery or “sakagura” (蔵)

Rice Milling & Polishin

When rice is harvested from a field, it is very similar to most other cereal grains like wheat, millet, barley, oats, or corn. The remaining percentage of rice is known as sei-mai buai (精米歩合). For most high-quality sake, it usually ends up being 40-85% remaining. Meaning 15-60% of the rice has been removed. This simple choice made by the Tōji on how much polishing to use, it will effect just about every stage of the process to come, impacting timing, temperatures, acidity, taste, aroma, composition, food paring, marketing, and many other aspects.
